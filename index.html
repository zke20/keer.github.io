Red Is Right
  • GENRE : Single player puzzle game
  • PLATFORM: HTML 5
  • POSITION: Independent producer
  • DATE: September 2023

The main theme of this game is censorship of art works on social media platforms. Player controls the Apple of Eve to switch art galleries in two parallel worlds, and step by step discover the tragic stories of the portraits and sculptures - they were created by human culture and then censored by humans on the grounds of a higher level of civilization.

Assassin's Creed Jade
  • GENRE : Open-world game
  • PLATFORM: iOS, Android, PC
  • ENGINE: Unreal Engine 4
  • POSITION: Open world level designer, technique designer
  • DATE: May 2020 - Feb 2023

This is the first open-world Assassin's Creed built for iOS and Android, which is set in third-century BCE China, following the Warring States period and the unification of China under the Qin dynasty. I contributed to this game's design and development through an internship and then formal employment at Tencent.

Drag Them All
  • GENRE :2D platform game
  • PLATFORM: PC
  • ENGINE: Unity
  • POSITION: Level designer, Art designer
  • DATE:Jul 2023

This is a game made in 48 hours for 2023 GMTK GameJam. The theme is "Roles Reversed". We imagine that it would be very interesting when the main character operation of traditional platform jumping games is disabled, and players outside the game console can only move by physically shaking and dragging the game console.


As part of a three-person team, I handled all of the art design and some level creation for this game. I designed the sleepy protagonist, a physical game console interface that rotates with the screen rotation to increase interactivity, and clever hidden easter eggs.

Honor of Kings: New Dimension Journey
  • GENRE :Immersive music game
  • PLATFORM: iOS , Android
  • ENGINE: Unreal Engine 4
  • POSITION: Level designer
  • DATE:Jul 2022

This is a 15-minute game that is highly integrated with music and includes interactive gameplay and free exploration. I was involved in this game development during my formal employment at Tencent.

This is part of my undergraduate graduation project and an art activity I designed for the Tsinghua University Cloud Campus Celebration. It was originally planned to take place on the lawn in front of the school auditorium, and more than 20 participants were invited to draw the posture they wanted to die in and the epitaph they wanted to leave. Affected by the new coronavirus epidemic, the event was not held offline. So, I integrated the participants' works into a painting.

The University of the Philippines ran naked together in celebration of its centennial anniversary, and the scene was heated. In conservative societies, "streaking" always seems to be associated with shame, rebellion and even crime.

Death remains a cultural taboo in China. Both before or after the outbreak of the coronavirus, death is represented either as statistics or highly sanitized rituals. But does this stigma around death reflect its true nature? Perhaps open conversations about mortality could reveal its inherent humanity.

When the lawn in front of the auditorium becomes our cemetery, Do you dare to take off your clothes, face your sincere self calmly, and give your death a special commemoration? A "dead-line" divides the grass into countless areas, inviting participants to draw the outline of their favorite "death" and leave a sentence of what they want to say most at that moment. When night falls, these words will be displayed on fluorescent materials. The school anniversary is over, and the only ones standing here are the classmates who are still in the garden. These anonymous words are thought-provoking
